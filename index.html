About me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate studying computational statistical physics and expecting to graduate September 2015. In graduate school I studied the physics of disordered systems, in which complex collective behavior often emerges from simple-looking models.

During my graduate studies I have developed analytical and computational skills useful for exploring large data sets and extracting meaningful insights. Recently I've become interested in machine learning, especially deep learning and neural networks, which share some intriguing features with the “spin glass” models I have been studying as part of my Ph.D. thesis.

After finishing my Ph.D. I plan to pursue a career in data science and/or machine learning, where I'm excited to apply the insights and analytical skills I have developed studying disordered physical systems to a broader range of problems.
